Who should take this course?

People who :

  • People with a scientific university degree wishing to integrate the cosmetics field
  • Cosmetic Chemists, pharmacists, engineers
  • Research and Development personnel
  • Compounding personnel manufacturing cosmetics
  • Raw material suppliers in the cosmetic industry
  • Product development personnel in sample development of cosmetic products
  • Technical staff involved in practical development and/or manufacturing of cosmetic products
  • Those wanting a Professional training program for their own cosmetic brand

Equipment you will need for beginner cosmetic science workshops:

In order to study the beginner cosmetic science course you will need to purchase or have access to standard laboratory equipment; or the following basic equipment if studying from home:

  • scales that measure up to at least 1.2kg in 0.1g increments
  • 2 x Beakers 250ml, 2 x Beakers 500 ml
  • 3 x 1.1L stainless steel mixing bowls
  • 1 x 8 wire whisk (250 – 300mm)
  • 2 hot plates (or normal stove top)
  • Hand mixer
  • Thermometer

Optional: practical kit with cosmetic ingredients to make all formulas in this course - please purchase separately here

All the end of my course, students will be able to

  • Develop safe, stable and effective cosmetic formulas for a range of personal care products
  • Provide technical documents to support the safety, stability and performance claims of a cosmetic product
  • Develop cosmetic formulas from a product development brief and communicate necessary changes
  • Accommodate industry innovations into cosmetic formulations without impacting on the stability
  • Reverse engineer and/or adapt cosmetic formulations to suit company requirements
  • Satisfy safety and regulatory obligations in the development of cosmetic formulas and personal care products
